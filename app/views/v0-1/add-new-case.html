Submit a pipeline for spend assurance

Central government organisations, including departments and the bodies they sponsor, must obtain Cabinet Office approval when they want to spend money on specified activities.

Who is this service for?

This service is for Departments to submit their pipeline of planned activities to Cabinet Office to gain the necessary approval to spend this money.

You will be asked a number of questions relating to your spend. These questions will be dependent on the type of control you select.

This service currently supports the Digital & Technology, and Commercial assurance process. If you require assurance for spend in other areas, please see Cabinet Office controls.

Email cabinetofficecontrols@cabinetoffice.gov.uk for general questions about the spend controls process and policy.

Things to consider

You will need to provide details regarding your spend, such as a description of the spend, the type of spend, anticipated value of the spend and the anticipated end date.

You are not required to complete all of this information in one go and can add further information as and when available.

Tech code of practice questions

The technology code of practice (TCoP) is a cross-government agreed standard used for the Cabinet Office spend control process and the Local Digital Declaration.

You should use the TCoP for all of your technology projects or programmes. Consider each point, align your project or programme with the mandatory points, and follow as many of the remaining points as is practical. You’ll get the most benefit by aligning your organisation’s technology and business strategies to the Technology Code of Practice.

Specific sections

  1. Define user needs
  2. Make things accessible and inclusive
  3. Be open and use open source
  4. Make use of open standards
  5. Use cloud first
  6. Make things secure
  7. Make privacy integral
  8. Share, reuse and collaborate
  9. Integrate and adapt technology
  10. Make better use of data
  11. Define your purchasing strategy
  12. Make your technology sustainable
  13. Meet the Service Standard

For more information, read the guidance on defining user needs on GOV.UK.
